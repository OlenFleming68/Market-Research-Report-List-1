<p><p>Laser Doppler Velocimetry (LDV) is an advanced technique used to measure the velocity of fluid flow by analyzing the frequency shift of scattered laser light from moving particles in the fluid. The market is divided into two main types: Forward Scatter Receivers, which detect light scattered in the same direction as the incident beam, optimizing measurement sensitivity for high-speed flows; and Backward Scatter Receivers, which capture light scattered in the opposite direction, providing better signal quality for lower velocities.</p></p>
