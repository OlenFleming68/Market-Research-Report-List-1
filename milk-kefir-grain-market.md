<p><p>The milk kefir grain market is categorized into two main types: dehydrated and non-dehydrated. Dehydrated milk kefir grains are processed to remove moisture, extending shelf life and allowing for easier transport and storage. They require rehydration before use. In contrast, non-dehydrated grains are fresh and active, offering immediate fermentation benefits but with a shorter shelf life, needing proper refrigeration. Both types cater to different consumer preferences and usage scenarios in dairy fermentation.</p></p>
