<p><ul><li>Air Drying</li><li>Freeze Drying</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The dehydrated mushroom flakes market primarily includes two types of drying methods: air drying and freeze drying. Air drying involves removing moisture from mushrooms using warm air circulation, resulting in a cost-effective but potentially less nutrient-retentive product. In contrast, freeze drying preserves the mushrooms by quickly freezing them and then removing moisture under vacuum, maintaining their flavor, color, and nutritional value. Both methods cater to different consumer preferences and applications in culinary and industrial sectors.</p></p>
