<p><p>Order Management Systems (OMS) come in two primary market types: on-premise and cloud-based. On-premise OMS are installed locally on a company's servers, offering greater control and customization but requiring significant IT resources for maintenance and updates. In contrast, cloud-based OMS are hosted on remote servers, providing scalability, accessibility, and lower upfront costs. This model enables businesses to access real-time data and collaborate seamlessly, making it a popular choice for companies seeking flexibility and reduced infrastructure burden.</p></p>
