<p><ul><li>Autoclave Process</li><li>Tubular Process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Low Density Polyethylene (LDPE) is produced through two primary processes: Autoclave and Tubular. The **Autoclave Process** uses high-pressure reactors to create LDPE with a highly branched molecular structure, ideal for flexible films and packaging. The **Tubular Process** employs tubular reactors under high pressure, yielding LDPE with better clarity and uniformity, suitable for high-performance applications like coatings and laminates. Both methods cater to diverse market needs, with Autoclave LDPE excelling in flexibility and Tubular LDPE in precision and consistency.</p></p>
