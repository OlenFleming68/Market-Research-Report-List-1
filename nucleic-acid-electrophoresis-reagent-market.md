<p><ul><li>Gel Electrophoresis</li><li>Capillary Electrophoresis</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The nucleic acid electrophoresis reagent market comprises two primary types: gel electrophoresis and capillary electrophoresis. Gel electrophoresis involves separating nucleic acids using a gel matrix, commonly agarose or polyacrylamide, facilitating size-based discrimination. It’s widely used for DNA and RNA analysis. In contrast, capillary electrophoresis utilizes thin capillaries filled with a gel or liquid to separate nucleic acids based on their charge and size, offering higher resolution and faster results, making it suitable for precise applications in genomics and clinical diagnostics.</p></p>
