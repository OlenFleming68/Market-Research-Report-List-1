<p><p>Montmorillonite clay, commonly known as bentonite, is categorized into three primary types based on its dominant cations: sodium bentonite, calcium bentonite, and potassium bentonite. Sodium bentonite is highly absorbent and widely used in drilling fluids and sealants. Calcium bentonite is often utilized in foundry sands and as a processing agent. Potassium bentonite, with its superior swelling ability, is primarily found in agricultural applications and serves as a soil conditioner. Each type caters to specific industrial and environmental needs.</p></p>
