<p><p>In the multi-layer parquet market, two notable types are glued and floating. The glued market refers to parquet flooring that uses adhesives to securely attach each wooden plank to the subfloor, providing stability and a seamless appearance. In contrast, the floating market involves planks that interlock or are installed over an underlay without direct attachment to the subfloor, allowing for easier installation and flexibility. Each type caters to different preferences in terms of installation methods and aesthetic outcomes.</p></p>
